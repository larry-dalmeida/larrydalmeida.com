## Summary 

The secret to beoming a highly valued engineer is to consistently: 

1. Blocking time to focus at work everyday.
2. Adding value by working on high value tasks that contribute to your team's goals.
3. Communicating the added value to your stakeholders and your manager.

## Focus on High Value work

- Take a look at your team's todo list and find the item that's most valuable to the goals of your team. 
- If you're not sure, ask your manager or team leader.
- Sometimes adding value might mean [gluework](https://fellow.app/blog/management/a-guide-to-understanding-glue-work/#:~:text=handle%20glue%20work-,What%20is%20glue%20work%3F,-Glue%20work%20describes) or doing something that's not the most interesting - if this is what your team needs now then this is what you should be doing, unless you can delegate it to someone who is more capable of doing it or has a learning opportunity.


## Block 2-4 hours for deep work everyday

- Setup a minimum 2 hour blocker on your calendar everyday to work on your most valuable task. 
- Your 'focus' hours should be non-negotiable. 
- Don't accept any meetings during this time. This may not always be feasible and overriding your focus time should be a rare acception.
- My focus time is between 8:00 AM to 10:00 AM every morning as that's usually when no one is expecting to meet or respond to chat messages.


## Communicate the value

- Once you've added value, it's time to make sure that the people who should know about it, actually know about it.
- Communicate it proudly to your manager, team and stakeholders. 
- Celebrate it during your daily standup and show it during your team's demos.

## Conclusion

As you add value and communicate it, you will be trusted with more important tasks.
This will in turn also help you grow and attract larger challenges. 
You will then have the opportunity to delegate parts of these larger challenges you are already familiar with to other less experienced engineers others and help them grow.
Trying to find hard and interesting problems yourself isn't scalable. Let the hard problems come to you.

Your peers will appreciate what you bring to the team and the business will reward you for it.
In the end, the business grows, your team grows and so do you. Everybody wins.
