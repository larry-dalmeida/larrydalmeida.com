The secret to beoming a highly valued engineer is to consistently: 

1. Add value by working on high value tasks that contribute to your team's goals.
2. Communicate the value added to your stakeholders and your manager.

## Focus on High Value work

Take a look at your team's todo list and find the item that's most valuable to the goals of your team. 
Get started on that. 
Anything else is a distraction.

## Practice identifying high value work

Sometimes adding value might mean gluework or doing something that's not the most interesting - if this is what your team needs now then this is what you should be doing.

An exception is if you should be delegating it. 
This is recommended if you're skills are best used on another important task and/or someone else on the team could learn from this task without risking the delivery timeline.
Focusing on higher value does not mean constantly firefighting.

Sometimes the highest value that needs to be added to the long term success of the team is by doing a task you've never done for your own growth and learning.
The more value you add, the more you'll be entrusted to work on the big, hairy and cool problems. 

Chasing hard, interesting problems isn't scalable. 
Let the hard problems come to you.

## Block 2-4 hours for deep work everyday

Setup a minimum 2 hour blocker on your calendar everyday to work on your most valuable task. 
Don't accept any meetings during this time.
Your 'focus' hours should be non-negotiable.

## Communicate the value

Once you've added value, it's time to make sure that the people who should know about it, actually know about it.
Communicate it proudly to your manager, team and stakeholders. 
Celebrate it during daily standups and talk about it during your team's demos.

## Conclusion

As you add value and communicate it, you will be trusted with more important tasks.
This will in turn also help you grow and attract larger challenges. 
You will then have the opportunity to delegate parts of these larger challenges you are already familiar with to other less experienced engineers others and help them grow.

Your peers will recognise that value and the business will reward you for it.
In the end, the business grows, your team grows and so do you.

Everybody wins.
