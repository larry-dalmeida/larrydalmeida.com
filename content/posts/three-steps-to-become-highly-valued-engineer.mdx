## Focus on high value work

Take a look at your team's todo list and find the item that's most valuable to the goals of your team. Get started on that. Anything else is a distraction.

Sometimes that might mean working on something that's not the most interesting. But don't let that distract you.
The more value you add, the more you'll be entrusted to work on the big, hairy and cool problems. 

Chasing hard, interesting problems isn't scalable. 
Let the hard problems come to you.

## Block 2-4 hours for deep work everyday

Setup a minimum 2 hour blocker on your calendar everyday to work on your most valuable task. 
Don't accept any meetings during this time.
Your 'focus' hours should be non-negotiable.

## Communicate the value

Once you've added value, it's time to make sure that the people who should know about it, actually know about it.
Communicate it proudly to your manager, team and stakeholders. 
Celebrate it during daily standups and talk about it during your team's demos.

## Conclusion

If you feel odd about all this visibility, remember that you're not doing it just for yourself.
As you add value and talk about it, you will inspire other engineers to step up.
You will also grow and attract more challenges which will inturn allow others to step up to tackle the problems you were tackling before.
Everybody wins.
